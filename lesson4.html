Farming

Farming involves rearing of animals and crop cultivation; it is an important part of agriculture.

Types of Farming

Agricultural crops turned into products fall into one of four groups: foods, fuels, fibers, or raw materials. Roughly 11% of the planet's land is dedicated to crop production, and close to 26% is being used for animal pastures.

Extensive Farming

When more land is brought under cultivation in order to increase output, it is termed as extensive cultivation or extensive farming. In extensive farming it is the only land, which is increased to get more yield, other factors remain unchanged.

Intensive Farming

Under such farming, in contrast to extensive farming, more labour and capital is used in the same plot of land to get more yields.

Back to Top

Arable Farming

Arable farming involves growing of crops only in warm climate. It is practiced on gently sloping or flat land with deep, fertile soil.

Back to Top

Specialized Farming

The farm from which 50% or more income is derived from a single enterprise viz. crops, livestock, dairy, poultry, etc., such farm is called specialized farm and farming is called specialized farming.
